Welcome to Headache Relief

Common Headache Types:

  • Tension Headaches: Often described as a tight band around the head.
  • Migraine: Accompanied by nausea, visual disturbances, sensitivity to light or sound.
  • Cluster Headaches: Intense pain, usually centered around one eye.
  • Sinus Headaches: Caused by inflammation of the sinuses, with nasal congestion.

Headaches: Fever Grass Tea

Ingredients: Fever grass (lemongrass), hot water, honey (optional)

Background: Fever grass is used in Jamaica for its headache-relieving properties. The grass is steeped in hot water, with honey added for sweetness.

History: The use of fever grass in Jamaica for medicinal purposes dates back centuries, integrating African and East Indian traditional medicine.

Headaches: Eucalyptus Infusion

Ingredients: Eucalyptus leaves, hot water

Background: Eucalyptus leaves are used in Bolivia for headache relief. The aromatic oils released during steeping create a therapeutic infusion.

History: The use of eucalyptus in Bolivia is rooted in traditional Andean medicine, valued for its healing properties.

Headaches: Lavender Tea

Ingredients: Lavender flowers, hot water

Background: Lavender tea is used in Armenia for its headache-relieving properties, appreciated for its soothing aroma.

History: Lavender's calming and therapeutic effects have been valued in Armenian traditional medicine since ancient times.

Headaches: Green Tea Compress

Ingredients: Green tea leaves, hot water, cloth

Background: A compress made from green tea leaves is applied to the forehead in Japan to soothe headaches, leveraging the antioxidants and calming properties of green tea.

History: The therapeutic use of green tea in Japan extends back centuries, originally influenced by traditional Chinese medicine.

Headaches: Brahmi Oil Massage

Ingredients: Brahmi oil, base oil (like coconut or sesame oil)

Background: Brahmi oil is used in India for scalp massages to relieve headaches, known for its calming and stress-relieving properties.

History: Brahmi has been a part of Ayurvedic medicine in India for centuries, used primarily for its benefits in improving memory and reducing stress.
